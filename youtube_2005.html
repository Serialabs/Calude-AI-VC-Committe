Seed 2005 Video Platform

YouTube

Broadcast yourself — simple video sharing for everyone

Stage 1: Deal Memo Extraction

Problem
  • No easy way to upload and share video online
  • Existing solutions require plugins and technical knowledge
  • Video hosting is expensive
Solution
  • Simple upload-convert-community model
  • Flash-based player embeddable anywhere
  • User profiles and subscriptions
Business Model
  • No proven revenue model yet
  • Potential for advertising pre-roll
  • Premium content licensing
Stage
  • Seed — beta product launched, growing rapidly
Market Size
  • Online video market nascent but growing
  • Broadband penetration increasing
  • Media industry $500B+
Traction
  • Rapid user growth
  • Millions of daily views
  • Viral sharing mechanism
Potential Outcomes
  • Bull: Dominant video platform, media transformation ($10B+)
  • Bear: Bandwidth costs unsustainable, copyright lawsuits
Competition
  • Google Video
  • Vimeo
  • Traditional media portals
  • P2P sharing

Debate Triggers

  • Absence of revenue model
  • Copyright infringement risks
  • Massive bandwidth costs
  • Viral growth metrics
  • Platform potential for creators
