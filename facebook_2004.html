Launch 2004 Social Network

thefacebook

A verified online social network for college students

Stage 1: Deal Memo Extraction

Problem
  • Physical face books on paper, no way to connect across dorms
  • Limited social discovery at Harvard
  • No digital directory with real identities for college students
Solution
  • Verified online social network for college students
  • Real identity, profile-based connections
  • Digital face book with social features and discovery
Business Model
  • Advertising (display ads targeting college demographics)
  • Potential premium features
  • Verified user base enables precise ad targeting
Stage
  • Launch — live at Harvard
  • Expanding to other Ivy League schools
  • Early traction with rapid organic adoption
Market Size
  • 17M US college students
  • Potential global expansion
  • Adjacent social graph markets
Traction & Ask
  • 50% Harvard student signup within weeks
  • Organic viral growth, no marketing spend
  • Rapid expansion demand from other campuses
Potential Outcomes
  • Bull: Dominant social network ($100B+)
  • Bear: College fad that doesn't translate to mainstream
  • Depends on ability to expand beyond college demographic
Competition
  • Friendster
  • MySpace
  • College-specific sites, email/AIM

Debate Triggers

  • Big market in social networking
  • Privacy and social creep concerns
  • College-only may limit scale
  • No proven ad revenue model
  • Viral growth without marketing spend
