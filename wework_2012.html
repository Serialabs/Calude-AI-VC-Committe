Series A 2012 Real Estate Cautionary Tale

WeWork

Community-driven co-working spaces with beautiful design

Stage 1: Deal Memo Extraction

Problem
  • Traditional office leases are long-term and inflexible
  • Freelancers and startups need affordable workspace
  • Isolated working is unproductive
Solution
  • Community-driven co-working spaces
  • Beautiful design, flexible month-to-month terms
  • Networking events and community
Business Model
  • Lease arbitrage — sign long-term leases at wholesale, sublease desks at premium
  • Community membership fees
  • Revenue from events and add-on services
Stage
  • Series A
  • Multiple locations in NYC
  • Proven initial concept
Market Size
  • US commercial real estate $1T+
  • Flexible office segment growing at 20%+
  • Remote work trends accelerating demand
Traction & Ask
  • Several NYC locations operational
  • Thousands of members
  • Strong revenue growth but high burn rate
Potential Outcomes
  • Bull: Transform commercial real estate ($50B+)
  • Bear: Lease liability time bomb, unit economics never work at scale
Competition
  • Regus/IWG
  • Traditional office landlords
  • Home offices, coffee shops

Debate Triggers

  • Lease liability and capital intensity
  • Overvaluation risk
  • “Tech company” vs. real estate company
  • Community premium vs. commodity space
  • Rapid expansion vs. profitability
